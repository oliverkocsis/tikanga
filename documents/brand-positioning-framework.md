# Brand Positioning Framework

## Purpose

Define long-term perception in the market.

## Template: Brand Positioning

### 1. Category Definition

“We are a ______ company.”

### 2. Target Audience

Primary segment:

### 3. Core Problem

What systemic pain do we solve?

### 4. Value Proposition

Clear articulation of transformation.

### 6. Proof Points

* Customer logos
* Case studies / testimonials
* Product adoption metrics (activation, retention)
* Performance / reliability (uptime, latency)
* Security / compliance (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001)
* ROI outcomes (time saved, cost reduced, revenue impact)

### 7. Brand Personality Attributes

* 3–5 defining traits

### 8. Positioning Statement (Formula)

For [target], who struggle with [problem], we provide [solution] that delivers [outcome] unlike [alternative].

## How to Build It

1. Identify category norms.
2. Identify areas of sameness.
3. Choose deliberate differentiation.
4. Validate via messaging tests.
5. Embed across website, ads, and sales.

## Common Mistakes

* Generic claims.
* Too many differentiation pillars.
* Feature-based positioning.

# 6. Marketing Performance Dashboard

## Purpose

Provide leadership-level visibility into growth efficiency.

## Template: Marketing Dashboard

### 1. Revenue Metrics

* Revenue
* Marketing-sourced revenue
* Marketing-influenced revenue

### 2. Funnel Metrics

| Stage | Volume | Conversion | Target | Variance |
| ----- | ------ | ---------- | ------ | -------- |

### 3. Acquisition Metrics

* CAC
* CPL
* CPA
* ROAS

### 4. Retention & Expansion

* Churn rate
* Net Revenue Retention
* Expansion revenue

### 5. Unit Economics

* LTV
* LTV/CAC ratio
* Payback period

### 6. Channel Breakdown

| Channel | Spend | Leads | SQLs | Revenue | CAC |

### 7. Leading Indicators

* Website traffic
* Engagement rate
* Webinar registrations
* Demo requests

## How to Build It

1. Align definitions across org.
2. Automate data sources.
3. Separate leading vs lagging indicators.
4. Report monthly; review weekly.
5. Tie metrics to strategic goals.

## Common Mistakes

* Vanity metrics.
* Inconsistent definitions.
* No context or targets.
* Reporting activity instead of performance.
